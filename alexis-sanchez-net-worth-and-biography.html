Alexis Sanchez Biography

Alexis Sanchez love football started growing at a tender age, when he turned seventeen, he started playing for cobreloa where he made 47 appearances and scoring 12 times for the club before he was priced away by Udinese of Italy in 2006. He was later loaned twice to Colo-Colo and River plate in 2006-2007,2007-2008 respectively. During his two loan spells he helped the clubs to win their respective league titles. In 2011, Alexis Sanchez was sold to FC Barcelona of Spain, after playing 95 games for the club and scoring 20 times, at a fee of £25,milllion. His £25 million move to Barcelona made Alexis Sanchez the most expensive Chilean Footballer of all time.

After joining the Spanish giants in 2011, Alexis went on to establish himself as a regular starter in the front three along side Lionel Messi and David Villa. He helped the club to claim the LA Liga title during the 2012/2013 campaign. In 2014, Alexis moved to Arsenal after playing 88 times and scoring 39 goals for the Spanish giants, Barcelona.

Alexis Sanchez became an integral part of the club, as he helped them to emerge winners of the FA Community Shield and two FA cup title where he scored the finals. After some time he started losing his mark, and gradually fell down the pecking order before he was sold on swap deal to Manchester United in 2018 with a weekly pay of £500,000. Alexis couldn’t live up to expectations as he only managed three goals in 32 appearances which made the club loan him to Inter Milan before making the move permanent in 2020. So far he has been in sensational form for the Italian side while still battling with injuries sometimes.

At the international level, Alexis Sanchez has won two Copa America titles for his country, and has made 136 appearances, scoring 45 times for the Chilean national team.
