Career

In 1967, he played a minor role in Wait Until Dark thriller before making his debut in Broadway in the Rothschilds musical in 1970. Later on, he made appearances in Happy Days sitcom and Search for Tomorrow soap. In 1977 he starred in One One, a sports drama which he co-wrote with Jerry Segal, his father. In 1978, he was the star in The Death of Richie movie before co-starring in Ice Castles and The End

In 1981, he was co-starring the film The Chosen which got mixed reviews. He has appeared in several movies and TV shows that have gone to do well commercially. Besides acting and directing, Benson is a professor, teaching Tisch School of the Arts at New York University, University of South Carolina, and the University of Utah.

Awards & Achievements

His career in the film industry hasn’t gone unnoticed with Benson being nominated in several awards, of which some of them he has won. Some of his work has seen some harsh critic with movie critics rating them poorly. Stinker Award has nominated him in several of its awards, winning a total of three. Among stinker awards, he has won Worst Fake Accent twice in Male category and worst song-performance in a film.
