Career

Many might say that the reason Marco Andretti is speeding up to top gear is that he was born into a family known for its legacy in car racing, but that’s not it.

Andretti raced in the Star Mazda series in 2005. At that time, he was a high school student. He won three times at the Indy Pro Series- at St. Petersburg (Florida), the Liberty Challenge (Indianapolis), and Sonoma Raceway (located at Sears Point in the southern Sonoma mountains at Sonoma, California).

On May 28,  2006, Marco participated in the Indianapolis 500 and won the Rookie of the Year. On February 7 and February 8, 2007, Andretti joined in the Honda Racing F1 for the second time, after test driving their Formula One for the first time on December 16, 2006. On August 28, 2007, 19-year-old Marco Andretti won the Indy Car League at the Infineon Raceway in Sonoma, California.

His career took a significant turn after that victory. After an unsuccessful 2007, Andretti started getting back on track from 2008 after winning his first IndyCar pole series at the Milwaukee Mile. Andretti’s best finish in 2009 is at Texas, where he ranked fourth. He participated in the IndyCar series In June 2011 and won it at Iowa Speedway.

2013 proved out to be a progressive year for him, where he consistently remained in the top 5 throughout the race at the Indy 500 car racing series and was qualified at the top 10 at the Toronto doubleheader of the competition. In 2018, Andretti switched his team to No.98 from No.27, exchanging with car racer Alexander Rossi.
