Time star Stephen Graham went to extreme lengths to prepare for role in gritty BBC drama

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, September 7, 2024

The actor plays Eric McNally in the new series

    Time on BBC is the brilliant new drama everyone is talking about. With an emotional storyline and incredible performances from its impressive cast, it's no wonder that Jimmy McGovern's latest offering is being praised by fans and critics alike.

    MORE: Stephen Graham's wife Hannah stars alongside him in BBC's Time - did you spot her?

    And it seems that the cast of the show put the work in to ensure full dedication to the role. Stephen Graham, who plays prison officer Eric McNally, revealed in a recent interview that to prepare for his stint in the three-part series, he spent time with a real-life prison officer who had 30 years of experience in the job.

    WATCH: Time starring Sean Bean and Stephen Graham is on BBC now

    The actor told the Telegraph: "I absorbed this fella like a sponge. These prison guards, they're trying to do a job to the best of their ability. Their job is to protect themselves, protect the prisoners and make it a nice, reasonable, cohesive place where everyone can do their time, treated with respect."

    He continued: "[But] Nine times out of 10, they're not qualified to deal with people like this... I think that unfortunately, there's a lot expected of them in those circumstances."

    In, Stephen's character Eric is known as a firm but fair officer, nicknamed 'Boss' by the inmates. He soon strikes up a bond with Sean Bean's character, Mark Cobden, but Eric is facing his own problems within the prison system.
