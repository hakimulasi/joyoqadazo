Jimmy Jones Full Overview and Wiki

The Unconventional Path to Stardom

Albert Simmonds, popularly known as Jimmy Jones, embarked on an extraordinary journey from aspiring priest to becoming one of Britain’s most outspoken and successful stand-up comedians.

Born on February 9, 1938, in Southampton, England, Jones’s early life was far removed from the glitz and glamour of showbiz.

His transition from cabaret singing to comedy in 1962 marked the beginning of an illustrious career that would defy conventional paths to celebrity.

A Legend in Live Comedy

Jones’s reputation for risqué and non-PC humor set him apart in the comedy world. His catchphrase, kin ‘ell!, became a hallmark of his performances, endearing him to audiences while skirting the boundaries of television censorship.

This unique brand of humor, coupled with his engaging stage presence, propelled Jones to become a staple in entertainment pubs and rock venues, earning the admiration of not just the public but also fellow artists and bands like Iron Maiden and The Rolling Stones.

Pioneering Comedy on Video

In an era when television was the primary medium for comedians, Jones blazed a new trail by focusing on live performances and comedy videos.

Titles like Live At Kings Club and Live from the Talk of East Anglia not only sold in the thousands but also became highly collectible, cementing Jones’s status as a comedy icon.

The Financial Implications of Avoiding TV

Jones’s strategic avoidance of television, a medium then considered essential for a comedian’s success, did not hinder his financial prosperity. Instead, it allowed him to control his content and connect directly with his audience, a move that proved lucrative.

His live performances, often numbering up to 275 nights a year, along with the sales from his comedy albums and videos, contributed significantly to his net worth.

This direct engagement model highlighted the potential for comedians to thrive outside traditional media channels.

Legacy and Influence

Jones’s influence extends beyond his financial success. His career has inspired a generation of comedians, including Jim Davidson, who cites Jones as a major influence.

Despite the controversies surrounding his humor, Jones’s ability to draw crowds and his pioneering use of comedy videos have left an indelible mark on the industry.

FAQs about Jimmy Jones

Who is Jimmy Jones?

He is a British stand-up comedian, known for being outspoken. Born as Albert Simmonds on February 9, 1938, in Southampton, England, he has been active in comedy since 1962.

What is Jones’ early life and background?

He grew up in Rainham, Essex, attended a Catholic school, and initially wanted to become a priest. Jones moved into comedy after his early education.

When did Jones start his comedy career?

He began his comedy career in 1962, first appearing on stage at the Dagenham Working Men’s Club.

What are some milestones in Jones’ career?

He gained significant recognition after being discovered by Granada Television in a Walthamstow pub in 1970, leading to appearances on The Comedians.

In 1983, Jones received what is reported to be the first gold disc for a comedy album. He was also known as the first stand-up comedian to release a video of his live shows.

Did Jimmy Jones have any royal fans?

Yes, he had royal admirers, including Princess Margaret and Prince Charles. He became a favorite in royal circles after Keith Emerson helped him secure a late-night comedy room at Mayfair nightclub Gulliver’s.

What is Jimmy Jones’ signature catchphrase?

His catchphrase is ‘Kin’ ell,’ a playful reference derived from footballer George Kinnell.

Has Jones ever been involved in any controversies?

Although specific controversies are not detailed, Jones is known for his blue comedy, which has been considered too risqué for television in the past.

How does Jones view modern comedy?

He appreciates comedians like Micky Flanagan who return to the down-to-earth style of comedy. He believes in the enduring appeal of hard-hitting working-class humor.

Is Jimmy Jones’ work available for streaming?
