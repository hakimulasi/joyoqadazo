Andy Landers (born October 8, 1952, in Maryville, Tennessee) is a former head coach of the University of Georgia Lady Bulldogs basketball team who has been in the position for more than 30 years. Andrea Lauren and Drew Joseph are his children from his previous marriage to Pam McClellan, which took place in 198Upon completion of the 2007–08 season, Landers had amassed an overall record of 789–246 (.762), including a 707–225 (.760) mark at the University of Georgia. Landers graduated from Friendsville (Tennessee) High School in 1970, and then went on to get a Bachelor of Science in Physical Education from Tennessee Technological University in 197Landers started his coaching career in 1975 at Roane State Community College, where he had an 82–21 record over four seasons before being hired as the first athletic director at the University of Georgia by Vince Dooley when he was 26 years old. With a 37–85 record in its first six seasons, the Lady Bulldog programme that Landers inherited had practically no financial resources to work with. Landers, on the other hand, guided the Lady Bulldogs to a 16–12 record in his first season, and by his fourth season in Athens, he had guided them to their first of five NCAA Final Four appearances. By 1985, the Lady Dogs had advanced to the National Championship game for the first time.

 He has won four National Coach of the Year awards and three SEC Coach of the Year awards since taking over at UGA. He has guided the Lady Dogs to 23 NCAA Tournament appearances, five Final Four appearances, seven SEC regular-season titles, four conference tournament championships, and 21 seasons with at least twenty victories. He has coached two Olympians (who have combined to win a total of six Gold Medals) as well as 11 Kodak All-Americans throughout his career. In 2000, Landers received the Coach of the Year award from the United States Basketball Writers Association (USBWA). Today, his 24.4 wins per season average places him fourth among all active Division I women’s basketball head coaches, and his 789 career victories place him fourth all-time in the sport. Of the fourteen Division I women’s basketball head coaches to achieve 600 victories, Landers did it in the shortest amount of time, winning 784 games and reaching the milestone in only 784 games. Landers was inducted into the Women’s Basketball Hall of Fame as a part of the ninth class of inductees (the class of 2007) in 200Landers reached the milestone of 1,000 career victories on February 24, 2013, when the University of Georgia Lady Dawgs defeated Ole Miss 73–54.
