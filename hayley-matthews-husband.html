Hayley Matthews Career

Hayley Matthews Career - At the age of roughly 9, Hayley asked the boys' cricket team coach at the People's Cathedral Primary School if she could join the squad. She was able to play this way thanks to the coach, and after around two years she was named the team's captain.She became the opening batter for the Harrison College under-13 squad, which had previously been all-boys. She led the squad as captain for the first time in her final season as an under-13 player.

At the age of roughly 16 and a half, Hayley made her World Twenty20 (and international) debut for the West Indies in September 2014. She hit 55 runs in the first game of a four-match series on her West Indies WODI debut in November 2014 against the powerful Australians. She maintained her hot streak throughout the series' final games, finishing with a team-best total of 241 runs.

She competed in many international meets and contests in the javelin throw event at this period, immediately before and during her early years of international cricket. She achieved success in the sport by winning the "Gold" medal in the under-18 division at the 2015 CARIFTA (Caribbean Free Trade Association) Games, the "Silver" medal in the under-17 and under-18 divisions at the 2013 and 2014 CARIFTA Games, and the "Bronze" medal at the under-18 division of the 2014 Central American and Caribbean Junior Championships in Athletics.
