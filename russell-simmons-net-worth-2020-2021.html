Early Life

Russell Wendell Simmons was born in Queens, New York on October 4, 1957, to his parents Daniel Simmons and Evelyn Simons. He grew up in Queens. His brothers are Rev Run and Daniel Simmons Jr.

He completed his schooling at August Martin High School in 1975. Following that Simmons went to Harlem’s City College of New York. In there, he met Kurt Walker, who advised him to be in the hip hop phenomenon.

In December 1998, Russell Simmons got married to Kimora Lee. The couple has two daughters named Ming Lee and Aoki Lee born in 2000 and 2002 respectively. They divorced in 2006. He is known as a Christian Yogi and respects all religions. He practices Transcendental Meditation and advocates veganism and Ahimsa.

In November 2017, Simmons was accused of sexual assault by Keri Claussen Khalighi in 1991. Many other women have also accused him of sexual abuse including Amanda Seales, Kelly Cutrone, Lisa Kirk, Natasha Williams-Blach, and Toni Sallie. He has also been charged with rape by Jennifer Jarosik, Drew Dixon, Tina Baker, Sherri Hines, and Jenny Lumet.

Career

In 1983, Russell Simmons became the founder of Def Jam along with Rick Rubin. Two years later, he became the co-producer and played in the film ‘Krush Groove.’ He has also co-produced the television series ‘Def Poetry Jam’ and ‘Def Comedy Jam’ on HBO.

With Def Jam, he released music for various artists like LL Cool J, Beastie Boys, and many others. The music label was later bought for $100 million by Universal Music Group and was renamed as The Island Def Jam Music Group.

In 1996, he was the co-producer of the film ‘The Nutty Professor,’ which was a commercial hit. In 2000, Simmons produced ‘Dude, Where’s My Car?’ and in 2010, he became the producer of ‘It’s a Funny Story.’

He started working for the company Rush Communications from 1999. He is currently the CEO of Rush Communications. Simmons is also an author and has penned several books like ‘Life and Def: Sex, Drugs, Money, + God’ and ‘Success Through Stillness.’

Awards & Achievements

In 1993, Russell Simmons was nominated for the CableACE for the Def Comedy Jam. In 2011, he was given the Excellence in Media Award at the GLAAD Media Awards. He was also conferred with the Vanguard Award at the 2009 Image Awards. In 2013, Simmons was bestowed with the Visionary Award at the PGA Awards. He has also received an Honorary Doctorate Degree in Humane Letters from the Claremont Lincoln University in 2014.
