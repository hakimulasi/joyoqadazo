The Meaning Behind The Song: WYTB by Cult of the Damned

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, September 3, 2024

WYTBCult of the DamnedSly Moon, Black Josh, King Grubb, Lee Scott & Tony BrokeThe Church Of (2021)September 6, 2021RapLee Scott

“WYTB” is a track by the hip hop collective Cult of the Damned, featuring Sly Moon, Black Josh, King Grubb, and Lee Scott. It was released on September 6, 2021, as part of their album “The Church Of”. The track was written by Sly Moon, Black Josh, King Grubb, Lee Scott, and Tony Broke, with production by Lee Scott.

Now, let’s dive into the meaning behind this captivating song. “WYTB” showcases the lyrical prowess of the artists, painting vivid pictures with their verses as they delve into various themes. The song addresses personal struggles, societal pressures, and the complex nature of human relationships.

The first verse, delivered by Sly Moon, touches on themes of self-destructive behavior and the pursuit of pleasure. His lyrics reflect a sense of rebellion and dissatisfaction with conventional norms. Lines like “I’m bad for you, I’m bad for myself” convey a recklessness and disregard for consequences. Sly Moon’s words paint a picture of someone on the fringes of society, unapologetically embracing their vices.

In the second verse, King Grubb takes the spotlight, exploring themes of escapism and the pursuit of artistic expression. He reflects on his struggles as an artist and the limitations imposed by society. The line “Mind your business ‘cos I am a witness” suggests a desire to speak his truth and challenge societal expectations. King Grubb’s lyrics showcase his ability to weave introspection and social commentary into his verses.

Black Josh enters the track with the third verse, delivering his signature gritty style and introspective lyrics. He acknowledges his flawed nature and the consequences of his actions. Lines like “I don’t give a fuck what your mans do” hint at a disregard for societal judgments and a focus on personal growth. Black Josh’s verses dive into the complexities of human relationships and the struggles of personal identity.

As a fan of Cult of the Damned, “WYTB” holds a special place in my heart. The song captures the raw energy and authenticity that the collective is known for. The dark, atmospheric production by Lee Scott provides the perfect backdrop for the artists’ introspective and thought-provoking lyrics.

The lyrics resonate with me on a personal level as they explore themes of self-discovery, rebellion, and finding one’s voice in a world that often stifles individuality. The artists’ ability to convey their emotions and experiences through their words is truly captivating. It’s a reminder that music has the power to connect us, allowing us to find solace in shared struggles and experiences.

“WYTB” is a testament to the artistry and creative depth of Cult of the Damned. The song goes beyond surface-level entertainment, inviting listeners to delve into their own reflections and interpretations. It serves as a reminder to embrace the complexities of life and to question the norms and expectations imposed upon us.

In conclusion, “WYTB” by Cult of the Damned is a captivating track that delves into the depths of human experiences. Its thought-provoking lyrics and atmospheric production make it a standout piece in their album “The Church Of”. The song serves as a reminder to embrace our true selves and to question the world around us. As an avid fan, this song has left a lasting impact on me, encouraging introspection and self-reflection.
