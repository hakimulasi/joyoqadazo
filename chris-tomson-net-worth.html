Early Days and Vampire Weekend’s Formation

At Columbia, Tomson’s encounter with Ezra Koenig, Rostam Batmanglij, and Chris Baio led to the formation of Vampire Weekend, a band that would later top charts and adorn festival line-ups worldwide.

The band’s self-produced debut album released shortly after their graduation was a sleeper hit, eventually leading to mainstream success. This initial success laid a financial foundation that would grow through subsequent albums like Contra and Modern Vampires of the City, each a critical darling and commercial success.

Diversification into Solo Projects

Understanding the whimsical nature of music industry success, Tomson didn’t just rest on his laurels. In 2017, he launched a solo project under the pseudonym Dams of the West.

His debut solo album, Youngish American, was not only an artistic venture but also a clever financial move, allowing him to tap into a different subset of the indie music market. This diversification exemplifies a strategic approach to career longevity and financial stability.
