Sami Switch Oh Why Vs Ed Sheeran Shape Of You, Some Recent Developments On Ongoing Lawsuit

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, July 7, 2024

Sami Switch Oh Why vs. Ed Sheeran's Shape Of You lawsuit battle has put the singers in rather an uncomfortable position. Meanwhile, Ed denies the copyright claims against the song released in 2015 before his.

Ed Sheeran is embroiled in a legal court battle with two songwriters who claim that sections of his 2017 hit Shape of You are copied from their song Oh Why.

According to Sami Chokri and Ross O'Donoghue 'Shape Of You' infringes on "certain lines and phrases."

Chokri released Oh Why in 2015 under the name Sami Switch.

Sami Switch Oh Why Vs. Ed Sheeran Shape Of You - Lawsuit Wikipedia

According to Sami Switch, Oh Why and Ed Sheeran's Shape Of You has similarities.

However, Ed denies the allegation saying he wasn't aware that the song existed. 

Andrew Sutcliffe QC, Chokri and O'Donoghue's attorney, argued that Sheeran treated lesser-known songwriters differently than famous ones.

On the other hand, Sheeran rejected the claims that he had cleared parts of songs with "a lot" of unknown musicians.

According to Chokri and O'Donoghue, Shape of You's "Oh I" hook is "strikingly similar" to a section of their song Oh Why.

Additionally, they alleged, Sheeran "borrows ideas and tosses them into songs, sometimes he will recognize it, sometimes he won't."

Sheeran has denied being a "magpie" who plagiarizes other people's work without credit.

He gave other examples of lesser-known musicians he has recognized, including Shivers and Visiting Hours.
