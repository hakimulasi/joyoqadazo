Mona Brorsson Wiki-Lifestyle-Career

Early Career

At the 2022 Olympic Winter Games in Beijing, she won gold in the relay. At PyeongChang 2018, she is the second-oldest female biathlete from Sweden. Despite missing six individual shots, the most of any athlete in the top-8, she won silver in the relay. She has now won one World Championship medal in six successive appearances, including relay silver in 2019. (Oestersund). Hanna Oberg, who was leading heading into the last leg, missed five shots, allowing Norway to pass Sweden and win by 24.3 seconds. Oestersund had her greatest individual performance of the season, finishing three of her four races in the top ten, including a career-high fifth place in the sprint.

She has nine World Cup podium finishes (all relay), the first of which came in January 2018 following Sweden's third-place performance in Oberhof, the country's first podium in the sport since 2011. Has two victories with the Oberg sisters, both in the 2020/21 season (Kontiolahti, Nove Mesto) (Hanna and Elvira). Individually, the best finish is fifth, with two second-place finishes in 2020/21. Her lone individual podium finish in a major competition was gold at the 2014 European Championships in Nove Mesto. Also competed in the 2013 Brezno edition. Two Junior World Championships were held in 2010 and 2011, as well as the 2013 Winter Universiade in Trentino.
