Who is Viktor Bout?

Viktor Bout is a Russian businessman and former Soviet military translator, widely known for his involvement in the international arms trade. His activities have earned him the nickname “The Merchant of Death”. Bout was arrested in 2008 in Thailand and later extradited to the United States, where he was convicted of numerous crimes related to arms trafficking.

Early Life and Career

Bout was born in Dushanbe, Tajik SSR, Soviet Union. He graduated from the Military Institute of Foreign Languages, a school that churns out elite military officers proficient in multiple languages. After the collapse of the Soviet Union, Bout started a small business and later ventured into the arms trade.

Arms Dealing Career

Bout’s arms dealing career began in the early 1990s. He reportedly supplied weapons to various factions in conflicts across Africa, the Middle East, and South America. His operations were vast and complex, involving numerous shell companies and aircraft to transport weapons.

Legal Troubles and Imprisonment

Bout’s activities eventually caught the attention of international law enforcement agencies. He was arrested in Thailand in 2008 following a sting operation by the U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA). In 2010, he was extradited to the United States, where he was convicted on charges including conspiracy to kill U.S. citizens and officials, delivery of anti-aircraft missiles, and providing aid to a terrorist organization.

Asset Seizure and Financial Impact

Following Bout’s arrest and conviction, his assets were seized by various governments. This included cash, property, and aircraft. The exact value of these assets is unknown, but it’s clear that this had a significant impact on Bout’s net worth.
